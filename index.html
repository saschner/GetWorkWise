What We Mean by Neurodivergent

The term neurodivergent was first used to describe people on the Autism Spectrum. Over time, the definition has grown to include those with ADHD/ADD, dyslexia, dyspraxia, Tourette's, and other cognitive differences that each affect how they process the world.

Workplace communication is full of invisible rules. For many neurodivergent professionals, it's not ability that holds them back—it's misunderstandings about tone, timing, or expectations.

  • Emails misread as blunt or cold
  • Struggles with self-advocacy
  • Overthinking every message
